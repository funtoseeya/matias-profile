A Minimal, Distraction-Free YouTube

Watching videos shouldn’t hijack your day

I love YouTube—but it doesn’t love me back. Endless feeds, rage-bait, and Shorts steal hours I could spend on things that matter. So I built my own version.

Reclaiming YouTube

Search, watch, repeat—no distractions

I kept finding myself trapped in the scroll, clicking on videos I didn’t really care about, losing hours, and leaving frustrated. YouTube’s algorithms aren’t built to help me—they’re built to keep me hooked.

So I stripped it down. My YouTube is just search and watch. No feeds. No Shorts. No manipulative recommendations. Just me and the videos I actually came to see. Finally, a YouTube that respects my time and attention.

The tech is quite simple. I use python to query Youtube's API and pass the search query, desired sorting, and whether you want to include shorts or not. I use vanilla JS for client-side (search, sort, refresh, render, etc.), and bootstrap for style.
